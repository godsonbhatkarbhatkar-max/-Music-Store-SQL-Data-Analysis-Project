Introduction

This project focuses on analyzing a Music Store database using SQL to generate meaningful business insights. The dataset includes customers, invoices, tracks, artists, genres, and purchase history. By querying and joining multiple tables, the project explores customer behavior, revenue trends, and music preferences. The analysis demonstrates how SQL can transform transactional data into actionable insights for strategic decision-making.

Problem

The music store generates large volumes of transactional data, but without structured analysis, it is difficult to understand:
Overall revenue performance and invoice distribution
Top 3 highest invoice values
Countries generating the highest number of invoices
The single best-performing city by total revenue
The top-spending customer globally
The Top 10 rock artists based on track count
The most popular genre in each country
The highest-spending customer per country
The business requires structured insights to improve marketing, promotions, and customer targeting.

Solution

Using SQL, the project answers key business questions by:
Identifying the senior-most employee based on job title
Determining countries with the highest invoice counts
Extracting the Top 3 invoice totals to analyze high-value transactions
Finding the highest revenue-generating city for promotional planning
Identifying the single highest-spending customer
Listing Rock music listeners ordered alphabetically
Ranking the Top 10 rock artists by total track count
Comparing track durations against the average song length
Calculating total spending per customer per artist
Identifying the most popular genre in each country
Determining the top customer in every country
Advanced SQL techniques such as JOINs, GROUP BY, subqueries, aggregations, and ranking logic were used to generate these insights.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how SQL can be used to analyze multi-table relational databases and answer real-world business questions. By identifying top customers, top 3 invoice values, top 10 artists, and country-level trends, the analysis supports data-driven marketing and strategic planning. It highlights strong SQL fundamentals and the ability to extract measurable insights from structured business data.

Key Insights

The analysis reveals that revenue is concentrated among a limited number of high-value customers and top-performing artists. Certain countries generate the highest invoice counts, while one city stands out as the top revenue contributor, making it ideal for targeted promotions. Rock music remains a dominant genre, with the Top 10 artists contributing significantly to overall track volume.

Additionally, comparing tracks above average duration helps understand content distribution, while identifying the top customer per country enables localized engagement strategies. Overall, the project transforms structured data into actionable business intelligence using SQL.
